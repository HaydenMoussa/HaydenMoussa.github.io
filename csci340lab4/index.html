What is the ISS

The ISS, or International Space Station, is a large, habitable spacecraft that orbits Earth. It serves as a space laboratory and research facility where astronauts from various countries conduct scientific experiments and research in microgravity. The ISS is a joint project involving multiple space agencies, including NASA (United States), Roscosmos (Russia), ESA (European Space Agency), JAXA (Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency), and CSA (Canadian Space Agency).
